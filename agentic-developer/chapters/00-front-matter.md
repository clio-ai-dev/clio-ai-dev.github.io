### What This Playbook Is

This is not a hype piece. This is not "AI will replace developers" clickbait.

This is a field guide. Five chapters pulled from the full 29-chapter book, chosen because they represent the most important ideas you need right now: what changed, why it matters, and how to actually do the work.

Here's the problem. Developers are adopting AI tools at a staggering rate. Anthropic's 2026 report says developers now use AI in 60% of their work. GitHub Copilot has generated over a million pull requests. Every IDE ships an "agent mode." The tools are everywhere.

But a landmark study from METR found that experienced open-source developers were actually 19% *slower* when using AI tools on familiar codebases. And here's the kicker: those same developers *believed* they were 20% faster.

That gap between perception and reality is where most developers live right now. They're using AI. They think it's helping. And in many cases, it's making them worse.

This playbook exists because the difference between those two outcomes, slower versus faster, isn't the tool. It's the technique. The developers who are genuinely more productive with AI agents aren't the ones who type the best prompts. They're the ones who provide the best context, write the best specs, and review the output with the sharpest eye.

They've become agentic developers.

### What You'll Learn

**Chapter 1: Software Development Has Changed** sets the stage. From autocomplete to autonomous agents, the evolution happened fast. This chapter traces it and explains why "just use Copilot" isn't a strategy.

**Chapter 2: The METR Paradox** digs into the study that should make every developer uncomfortable. AI can make you slower. Understanding why is the first step to making sure it doesn't.

**Chapter 3: Your New Job Description** redefines what it means to be a developer in 2026. You're not a typist anymore. You're an architect, a reviewer, and an orchestrator.

**Chapter 4: Writing Effective Context Files** (Chapter 6 in the full book) is the most practical chapter here. Context engineering is the single most important skill in agentic development. This chapter gives you templates, examples, and a system.

**Chapter 5: The TDD Agent Loop** (Chapter 13 in the full book) is a complete workflow. Write the tests. Let the agent write the code. Run the tests. Iterate. It's the highest-confidence way to work with AI agents, and this chapter walks through it start to finish with real code.

### A Note on Language

The code examples in this book use C# because that's what I write every day. But every pattern, every workflow, every principle applies to whatever language you use. If you write Python, TypeScript, Go, Java, or Rust, this book is for you. The agent doesn't care about your language. It cares about your context.
